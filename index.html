Prioritizing What Makes Us Human

Creating the foundations for an interdisciplinary field in an Age of AI, Polarization, and a Crisis of Meaning

Inaugural Field Building Convening • June 16–18, 2026

In a world rapidly reshaped by artificial intelligence, deepening polarization, and a growing crisis of meaning and connection, schools continue to emphasize achievement, performance, and outcomes—college, career, success—while many young people feel increasingly disconnected from themselves, from one another, and from a deeper sense of purpose in their lives.

In response to this moment, building on more than a decade of success serving thousands of NYC public high school students and engaging hundreds of educators through the QUESTion Project, we aim to co-create the foundational elements of a new interdisciplinary field centered on What Makes Us Human.

We will host the first convening at the Fetzer Institute June 16–18, bringing together leaders from across fields to begin shaping shared language, a unifying framework, guiding principles, and strategy.

What We Will Accomplish Together

This convening will bring together a focused group of leaders who sense that something essential is calling to be built. At a moment of profound cultural and technological change, we will explore why this work matters now, how each of us is uniquely positioned to contribute, and what becomes possible when we intentionally come together to build a new field. Through structured and open co-exploration, we will align around a shared mission, vision, and strategy—stretching our thinking, naming tensions, and shaping a bold, coherent narrative for What Makes Us Human.

We will move from vision to action—dreaming without limits while grounding our work in practice and rigor of inquiry. Together, we will outline some guiding principles, common misconceptions, and how this field create impact across multiple domains of learning and growth.

Together, we aim to:

  • Articulate a compelling, shared vision for a new interdisciplinary field
  • Co-create foundational principles that can guide practice and innovation
  • Illuminate how What Makes Us Human can transform learning across domains
  • Identify bold priorities and next steps to steward the field’s growth

A Place for Connection & Co-Creation

We’re grateful to gather at the Fetzer Institute in Kalamazoo, Michigan—an environment designed for reflection, relationship, and long-horizon thinking.

Travel, food & accommodation are complimentary due to the generosity of the Fetzer Institute and the Eric & Wendy Schmidt Foundation. Attendance is by invitation only.
