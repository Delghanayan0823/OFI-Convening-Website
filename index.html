Overview

What do young people—and those who support them—need to reclaim and evolve connection in an age of AI? This convening is designed to surface shared insight, strengthen coherence, and catalyze practical next steps.

Today’s young people are coming of age amid rising loneliness, anxiety, and polarization. Technology has accelerated these trends—pulling attention to screens, reshaping identity and belonging, and complicating what “real” means. Generative AI raises the stakes even further: it blurs authenticity, changes trust, and introduces new forms of relationship through technology—and with technology itself.

But it’s not only a crisis. It’s a crossroads. Young people are also already forging new ways to use digital spaces for expression, belonging, and community. The question is: how do we equip them with the inner and relational capacities to choose a future where technology strengthens relationships rather than replaces them?

What we aim to accomplish

This convening is designed as a space for collective discernment—strengthening coherence, surfacing alignment, and clarifying the most meaningful next steps for field building.

Goals for the convening
  • Align around mission + approach (vision, framework, strategy, and metrics) and resolve key inconsistencies.
  • Advance foundational content: guiding principles, common misconceptions, and concrete applications across domains (mental health, character, purpose, belonging, SEL, agency, and more).
  • Increase intellectual coherence by mapping convergences across existing frameworks and clarifying shared priorities.
  • Explore a system-change strategy and inroads across culture and domains of learning and growth.
  • Build coalition + stewardship: clarify levels of engagement and commitments to carry the work forward.
  • Define next steps: short- and mid-term priorities, working groups, and early outputs.
Your role as a participant
  • Bring your experience and perspective to strengthen the approach and help lay the foundations of this new field.
  • Join a community of stewardship for a nascent interdisciplinary effort.
  • Engage the theoretical framework that anchors what makes us uniquely human—and help refine it.
  • Co-develop the strategy for expanding this work through collaboration across disciplines and domains.

Agenda (high-level)

A simple arc: arrive → orient → explore → weave → commit.

Day 1 — Arrival & grounding

Arrive in the late afternoon. We’ll begin by building community and rooting ourselves in why connection matters— to us, to young people, and to this moment in time.

Day 2 — Sensemaking

Explore how AI and broader cultural forces are reshaping identity, trust, and relationship. We’ll map what supports connection—and what erodes it—across settings that matter (schools, families, communities, digital life).

Day 3 — Integration & next steps

We’ll weave insights into shared language and concrete pathways: priorities, collaborations, and early outputs (principles, research questions, prototypes, stories, or convening follow-ons).
