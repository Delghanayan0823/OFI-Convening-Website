Context

“In a world rapidly reshaped by artificial intelligence, deepening polarization, and a growing crisis of meaning and connection, schools continue to emphasize achievement, performance, and outcomes—college, career, success—while many young people feel increasingly disconnected from themselves, from one another, and from a deeper sense of purpose in their lives.

In response to this moment, building on more than a decade of success serving thousands of NYC public high school students and engaging hundreds of educators, we aim to co-create the foundational elements of a new interdisciplinary field centered on What Makes Us Human.

We will host the first convening at the Fetzer Institute June 16–18, bringing together leaders from across fields to begin shaping shared language, a unifying framework, guiding principles, and strategy.” —Convening framing

Your Contribution & What We Aim to Accomplish

This convening brings together leaders who sense that something essential is calling to be built. At a moment of profound cultural and technological change, we are here to explore why this work matters now, how each of us is uniquely positioned to contribute, and what becomes possible when we intentionally come together to build a new field. Through open-ended co-exploration, we will align around a shared mission, vision, and strategy—stretching our thinking, naming tensions, and shaping a bold, coherent narrative for What Makes Us Human.

We will move from imagination to action—dreaming without limits while grounding our work in practice. Together, we will shape guiding principles, challenge misconceptions, and map how this field can live across the full ecosystem of learning and human development. Through synthesis, system-level thinking, and coalition building, we will begin to chart a path for long-term impact—one that honors the depth of our humanity and expands what education can become.

Together, we aim to:

  • Articulate a compelling, shared vision for a new interdisciplinary field
  • Co-create foundational principles that can guide practice and innovation
  • Illuminate how What Makes Us Human can transform learning across domains
  • Identify bold priorities and next steps to steward the field’s growth
