Context & Goals

In an age increasingly defined by artificial intelligence, technological acceleration, and a culture of achievement, education faces a defining challenge: how to nurture what makes us human. While “college, career, and success” matter, their dominance has shifted learning toward outer performance at the expense of the inner dimensions that give life coherence and meaning.

We are living through a crisis of connection—reflected in rising anxiety, isolation, and polarization—that signals a developmental gap. Many wellbeing approaches focus on parts (skills, traits, competencies) without engaging the underlying wholeness that is foundational to healthy development. We need shared language and an actionable framework that approaches wholeness concretely through connection to self, others, and life.

This initiative places “What Makes Us Human?” at the center of educational thought and practice—advancing a pedagogy of connectedness that engages students’ whole selves. By bringing together educators, researchers, practitioners, and thought leaders, we aim to confirm shared language and intention, identify priority areas for collaboration, and generate momentum for a broader coalition and next steps to shape the field forward.

Vision A world where education cultivates people deeply connected to themselves, others, and life—acting with wisdom, compassion, and agency.
Mission Place what makes us human at the center of education by fostering connection to self, others, and life—co-creating frameworks, principles, and actionable guidelines.

What we’re going to do

This is a working convening to align around mission, vision, framework, strategy, approach, and metrics—and to advance the content needed to steward a nascent field.

1) Align on shared foundation Stress-test and refine the core framing: connection to self, others, and life (and the agency that animates it).
2) Develop practical content Review and further develop guiding principles, surface common misconceptions, and draft applications across domains (mental health, character, purpose, belonging, identity, SEL, etc.).
3) Build momentum & coalition Identify inroads for culture and practice, clarify participant contributions, and map additional partners to involve after the convening.
